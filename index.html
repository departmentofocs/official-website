Seaport Village Development

In his insightful piece, Matt Dulansky (our fisheries correspondent and quantitative team member) critically examines the Seaport San Diego redevelopment project, a transformative $3.6 billion endeavor reshaping San Diego's waterfront. He deftly navigates the intricate balance between modern urban development and the city's storied fishing heritage, shedding light on the local fishermen's apprehensions and the broader environmental concerns. Dulansky's analysis underscores the delicate dance between economic progress and the preservation of a community's cultural and ecological fabric in the face of sweeping urban change.

11/30/23

Marine Conservation Survey

Brendon Fezzey, our cheif data specialist, recently conducted a survey encompassing 145 UCSD undergraduate students, shedding light on their perspectives toward global issues. Notably, marine conservation emerged with comparatively lower perceived significance and self-reported knowledgeability among the surveyed topics. These findings suggest a possible trend indicating an underestimation and limited understanding of the importance of marine conservation among the student body. As a result, Fezzey's department aims to intensify efforts in advocating for the significance of marine conservation on a broader scale.
